# Strategy Notes

These are compact heuristics for a model playing through MCP.
They are intentionally short and biased toward safe, repeatable play.

## Route Planning

- Plan the route before clicking a map node.
- In Act 1, try to fight at least one elite when HP, potions, and deck strength support it.
- Keep a rest site before the boss when possible.
- Unknown rooms are flexible but high variance; use them more when the deck can tolerate randomness.
- Shops are strongest when you can remove a bad card, buy a major relic, or fix a weak matchup.

## Deckbuilding

- Do not take a card just because it is offered.
- Skip cards that do not solve a real problem.
- Early removal is strong because it makes draws more consistent.
- Build around a direction, but do not force a full archetype too early.
- Add scaling only after basic survival is covered.

## Combat

- Preserve HP; HP is a resource, but losing too much early ruins route flexibility.
- Potions are for damage prevention, elite fights, bosses, and lethal turns. Do not hoard them forever.
- In difficult fights, block plus weak is often better than greedy damage.
- Use frontload to survive early acts, then add scaling for bosses and longer fights.

## Silent-Specific Notes

- Silent starts with low HP, so early defense matters more than flashy synergies.
- Poison is a beginner-friendly scaling plan if you already have enough defense.
- Shiv and discard are strong, but only when payoff cards and relics appear.
- Draw and retention are valuable because Silent often wins by assembling the right turn, not by raw stats alone.

## Reward Flow Facts

- Normal monster rewards are gold plus card, with potion sometimes added.
- Elite rewards always include a relic.
- After Act 1 and Act 2 bosses, the flow is: gold/card rewards, then a special chest with three boss relics.
